Incorporation Of Entity
For setting up a business in India, an investor will need to decide which form of legal entity the business will take to start the operations. The favourable options for carrying out manufacturing/services and other related business operations are:
  • Private Limited Company - A Private Limited Company is a Separate Legal Entity, which offers Limited Liability to its Shareholders. The Shareholders are Limited and usually is closely held within the Group
  • Public Limited Company - A Public Limited Company is a Separate Legal Entity that offers Limited Liability. The Shares are widely held and offered to the General Public.
  • Limited Liability Partnership - Limited Liability Partnership is a Separate Legal Entity and is Liable to the full Extent of its Assets but the Liability of the Partners is Limited to their agreed Contribution in the LLP.

Additional Information
No minimum paid up capital, No filing for SPICe+ company incorporation form and filing of eMoA, eA0A. It takes ~ 18 days for the company incorporation process.
  • Pre-requirements for Company Incorporation
    • Minimum of two Persons should act as Directors and two Persons as Shareholders
    • Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association (Charter Documents) have to be Executed
    • Resolution to be passed by the Foreign Company’s Board for Incorporating a Company in India and Authorizing a Person to Sign on its behalf Duly Notarized and Apostilled
  • Company Incorporation
    • Simplified Single web based form SPICe+ to be filed online in order to Incorporate a new Company in India, which includes Processes such as Name Reservation, etc.
    • Digital Signature Certificates (DSC) to be Applied for Directors
    • Other Tax and Regulatory Registrations can be Processed through a Simplified form AGILE-PRO, and Bank Accounts may also be opened
    • The Registrar of Company will Issue a Certification of Incorporation on Successful Formation of the Company
  • Post Incorporation compliances
    • Appointment of Statutory Auditor should be done within 30 days of Incorporation. Board of Directors must Appoint a practicing Chartered Accountant to act as the Statutory Auditor.
    • Within 180 days of Incorporation, mentioned Capital in the Charter Documents has to be deposited in the Indian Bank Account and a Commencement Certificate needs to be obtained from the MCA.
IL/IEM Registration
Industrial Entrepreneur Memorandum (IEM)
  • Industrial undertakings exempted from the requirements of Industrial Licensing under Industries (D&R) Act, 1951 are required to file information relating to setting up of industries
  • Existing Units, New undertaking having an investment of Rs 10 Crore or above in the 'manufacturing sector' and Rs. 5 Crore or above in the 'services sector are required to file an IEM
  • Cannot be considered as a clearance /approval to carry on any industrial activity contemplated in the IEM unless the provisions of statutes/regulations/notifications etc., issued by the Central/ State Governments or any specific directions or Stay Orders issued by the Court/competent authority relevant to such an activity, are also fully complied with

Industrial Licensing (IL)
  • Licensing is done under Industries (Development & Regulation) Act 1951. Post 1991 de-licensing, presently only five industries require compulsory licensing :
    • Electronic Aerospace and Defence Equipment
    • Industrial explosives including detonating fuses, safety fuses, gun powder, nitrocellulose and matches 
    • Cigars and cigarettes of tobacco and manufactured tobacco substitutes
    • Specified hazardous chemicals i.e., (i) hydrocyanic acid and its derivatives (ii) Phosgene and its derivatives and (iii) Isocyanates & disocyanates of hydrocarbon not elsewhere specified (example methyl Isocynate)
    • Distillation and brewing of alcoholic drinks​
  • Any non-MSME unit manufacturing these items, need an IL which is issued with an obligation to export 50% of the new additional annual production to be achieved within a maximum period of 3 years​
